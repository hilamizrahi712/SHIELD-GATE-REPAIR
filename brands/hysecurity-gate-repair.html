What HySecurity Service Requires vs. Standard Gate Repair

HySecurity operators at secured facilities have service requirements that are different in kind from residential gate operators.

Fault Code Interpretation

HySecurity Smart Touch controllers log fault history. A trained technician reads the fault log, identifies pattern vs. one-time events, and addresses the root cause — not just the symptom that triggered the call.

Safety Device Testing

HySecurity operators are integrated with multiple safety devices. Any repair must include full functional testing of all connected safety inputs — not just verifying the gate moves.

Documentation

Secured facilities require written service records. We provide detailed documentation of every component tested, every adjustment made, and every part replaced — ready for your maintenance file.

Parts Quality

We use genuine HySecurity components or manufacturer-approved equivalents. Non-approved parts on a crash-rated HySecurity installation can void the rating. We don’t take shortcuts on security-critical hardware.

HySecurity Gate Repair FAQs

HySecurity Smart Touch controllers use fault codes to pinpoint the detected issue — everything from obstruction detection and loop detector faults to motor overcurrent and limit position errors. The fault code tells a trained technician exactly where to look. We look up the specific code for your model, test the indicated system, and address the actual cause. A common mistake is simply clearing the fault code without fixing the underlying issue — which just puts the system back in service until the next fault event.
HySecurity recommends semi-annual maintenance for high-cycle commercial applications and annual maintenance for lower-cycle installations. At a typical DFW commercial property with 100+ cycles per day, semi-annual maintenance keeps drive components in spec and catches board capacitor aging before it causes a failure. We offer scheduled maintenance programs for commercial HySecurity accounts.
Slow cycles and heat buildup on a HySecurity operator typically indicate the motor is drawing more current than normal — either because the gate is binding mechanically (alignment, rail debris, or roller wear on slide gate), the drive chain needs lubrication, or the motor itself is developing winding resistance due to heat age. We measure motor amperage draw and trace the source of the excess load.

Service includes hydraulic fluid level and condition check, solenoid valve inspection, cylinder seal assessment, and pressure testing.
