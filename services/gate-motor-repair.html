Gate Motor Repair in Dallas-Fort Worth

Gate motor running slow, making noise, or not running at all? Our technicians repair and replace all gate motor types across DFW — same-day service available.

Motor Repair Specialists

Every Gate Motor Type. Every Brand.

The gate motor — whether it's a linear actuator, underground hydraulic unit, or electromechanical operator — is the heart of your automatic gate system. When it fails, the gate fails. Our technicians have deep hands-on experience with every motor type and brand found across the DFW market.

Texas summers are especially hard on gate motors. Prolonged heat above 100°F accelerates internal brush wear, degrades motor insulation and pushes capacitors beyond their rated limits. We see this pattern constantly in DFW and know which components to inspect first — saving you diagnostic time and money.

Motor Types We Service

  • Linear actuator operators (swing gates)
  • Underground hydraulic operators
  • Electromechanical worm-drive operators
  • Rack-and-pinion slide gate motors
  • Articulated arm operators
  • Solar-powered gate motors
  • High-cycle commercial motors
  • Battery backup motor systems

Motor Problems We Repair

  • Motor not running (no power reaching motor)
  • Motor running but gate not moving
  • Grinding, squealing, or clicking sounds
  • Gate moving very slowly
  • Motor overheating and shutting off
  • Capacitor failure
  • Internal gear stripping
  • Motor brush wear
  • Worm drive failure
  • Actuator arm failure
Repair vs. Replace? We'll always tell you honestly. A capacitor swap on a good motor is $80–$150 and adds years of life. A burned-out motor with stripped gears should be replaced — and we'll tell you which brands offer the best longevity for DFW's climate so you make a smart investment.

Gate Motor Repair FAQs

If you hear a humming or clicking sound when the gate tries to open but nothing moves, the motor is likely getting power but the mechanical drive is failing. If there's complete silence (no sound at all), the issue is electrical — power supply, board, or wiring. Our technicians test both systems simultaneously to pinpoint the fault.
In DFW's climate, residential gate motors typically last 7–15 years depending on usage, brand quality and whether they're properly protected from direct sun. High-cycle commercial motors can last longer but wear faster per cycle. Regular maintenance significantly extends motor life.
